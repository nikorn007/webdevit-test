Loy Krathong Festival

The Festival of Lights

Paying respect to the Goddess of Water

History & Significance

Loy Krathong is one of Thailand's most enchanting festivals, celebrated annually on the full moon of the 12th Thai lunar month (usually November).

The name translates to "to float a basket," and comes from the tradition of making krathongs or buoyant, decorated baskets, which are then floated on a river.

  • Gratitude: To thank the Goddess of Water, Phra Mae Khongkha, for the water used throughout the year.
  • Forgiveness: To ask for forgiveness for polluting the rivers.
  • Letting Go: Floating the krathong symbolizes letting go of anger, hatred, and negative thoughts.

Festival Activities

Floating Krathongs

People gather at rivers, lakes, and canals to release their krathongs. It is believed that if your candle stays lit until it disappears out of sight, your wish will come true.

Yi Peng (Sky Lanterns)

In Northern Thailand (Chiang Mai), the festival coincides with Yi Peng, where thousands of paper lanterns are released into the sky, creating a mesmerizing view.

Beauty Contests

The "Noppamas Queen" beauty pageants are held in honor of the legendary figure Nang Noppamas, who is said to have created the first krathong.
